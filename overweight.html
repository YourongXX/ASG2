Here's what your body type says about you
Overweight and obesity are defined as abnormal or excessive fat accumulation that presents a risk to health. A body mass index (BMI) over 25 is considered overweight, and over 30 is obese. Today, more people are obese than underweight in every region except sub-Saharan Africa and Asia. It's a medical problem that increases the risk of other diseases and health problems, such as heart disease, diabetes, high blood pressure and certain cancers.
Why are some people overweight?
Obesity occurs when you take in more calories than you burn through normal daily activities and exercise. Your body stores these excess calories as fat. Especially in the United States(US), most people's diets are too high in calories as they consume a lot of fast food and high-calorie beverages eg. Mc Donalds, KFC , PizzaHut , Milkshake, Bubble Tea.
